K. L. Rahul

Indian Cricketer | Wicket-Keeper Batsman

Kannaur Lokesh Rahul, commonly known as KL Rahul, is a talented Indian cricketer born on April 18, 1992, in Bangalore. He is known for his stylish stroke play and solid technique. Over the years, Rahul has represented India in all three formats — Tests, ODIs, and T20s — and has adapted well to each role. Apart from being a top-order batsman, he also serves as a reliable wicket-keeper when needed. He has played key innings in important matches and often opens the batting or plays in the middle order. As of now, Rahul remains an important part of India's cricket setup and continues to perform in the Indian Premier League (IPL) as well.

Early Life & Education

Rahul spent his early years in Mangalore, completing his schooling at NITK English Medium School and pre-university at St. Aloysius College. At 17, he moved to Bangalore to pursue cricket, training at the Karnataka State Cricket Academy.

Cricket Career

Domestic Career

Rahul debuted in first-class cricket for Karnataka in the 2010–11 season. He scored 337 runs against Uttar Pradesh in the 2014–15 Ranji Trophy, becoming Karnataka's first triple-centurion.

International Career

  • Test Debut: December 26, 2014, vs. Australia
  • ODI Debut: June 11, 2016, vs. Zimbabwe
  • T20I Debut: June 18, 2016, vs. Zimbabwe

He is the first Indian to score a century on ODI debut and has multiple centuries across formats. Notably, he scored 199 against England in Chennai.

Major Achievements

  • First Indian to score a century on ODI debut.
  • Fastest fifty in IPL history (14 balls).
  • Only Indian batsman to score a century in first innings as opener in both Tests and ODIs.

Leadership Roles

Rahul has captained India in various matches and led IPL teams like Punjab Kings and Lucknow Super Giants, showcasing his leadership skills on and off the field.
